Southeast Asia Population Research Summary

As of 2023, the total population of Southeast Asian nations (ASEAN members) is approximately 677.6 million people. The region has experienced varying growth rates, with Singapore notably recording the highest population growth rate in Southeast Asia between mid-2022 and mid-2023 at 4.9%, primarily due to immigration.

The population is distributed across the ASEAN member states:
- Brunei
- Cambodia
- Indonesia
- Laos
- Malaysia
- Myanmar
- Philippines
- Singapore
- Thailand
- Vietnam

The region continues to be one of the most dynamically growing areas in terms of population and economic development, with significant demographic changes affecting urban development, workforce composition, and social structures.
